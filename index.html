Carnatic music

It is a south Indian classical music. We, Indians believe that music is originated from vedas and Goddess Saraswathi is the mother of music.

Music is also a form of worship in India. It is a medium to create social and philosophical awareness. In "Masana yetulorthune", Sri.Tyagaraja swami describes how we can control our mind and be productive. "Brahmamokkate", by Sri.Annamacharya is a classic example of a song that creates social awareness.

Online classes

With the advanced technology available and considering the current pandemic situations around the world, this option is a boon to music enthusiasts like us.

There are both single and group online classes. which are not very different. If you already have your friends and you all want to learn along, go with the group.


Students are taught both Carnatic(swarams) & Devotional(songs) music paralelly.

Duration of the class - 30 to 40mins per person.
Weekly 2 classes.
Monday, Thursday / Tuesday, Friday.

Periodical tests and programs will be conducted.

Offline classes in Stockholm.

It is so fun to learn in the classroom atmosphere. Especially children tend to procrasinate much lesser in the offline classes It also helps them understand the concept of Sruthi & Taalam easily. These create a healthy competition & motivation in the students.

Offline classes are taken in a group with 3 to 5 students in each group.
Students are taught both Carnatic(swarams) & Devotional(songs) music paralelly.

Duration of the class - 60min.
Weekly 2 classes.
Monday, Thursday / Tuesday, Friday.

Periodical tests and programs will be conducted.

Fee structure

The fees depend on the type of class(online, offline) you choose and the timezone you live in.

Irrespective of the type of class and time zone,
Fees is paid per month in advance. Not per class.
